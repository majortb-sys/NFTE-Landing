Golden Eagles, Golden Opportunity

Two Marquette graduates.
A decade of transforming lives.

Jane Walsh (Arts '94) and Mike Jortberg (Journalism '87) never met on campus — they graduated seven years apart. Yet both left Marquette carrying the same Jesuit call to service: Be The Difference. Decades later, their paths converged at NFTE, where together they've changed the trajectory of thousands of young lives.

1987
Mike Jortberg
A Journalist Graduates — and Starts Telling New Stories

Mike earns his Journalism degree from Marquette — the same year NFTE is founded — and begins building a career in business and technology consulting. His journalism instincts to seek untold narratives would later lead him to young entrepreneurs building businesses against the odds.

Jane Luthy Walsh crosses the stage at Marquette's commencement with a B.A. in History. Her ability to see the long arc of social change and how institutions create lasting impact becomes the foundation of a career dedicated to nonprofit advancement and mission-driven fundraising.

A Decade of Partnership

When corporate purpose meets community need

The Slalom–NFTE partnership began in Chicago and has since expanded to five regions nationwide — DC, New England, Mid-Atlantic, Midwest, and West. Slalom consultants coach students on business plans, model professional communication, and mentor young entrepreneurs one-on-one.

Most innovatively, Slalom Chicago and NFTE collaborated on using AI and machine learning to create virtual mentoring — ensuring no student is left behind due to distance or scheduling.
